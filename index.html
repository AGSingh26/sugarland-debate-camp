Summer Middle School Debate Seminar

Hosted by Ayaan Singh & Michael Meng | August 4–8, 2025

About the Seminar

Designed for rising 7th–9th graders, our virtual seminar introduces students to the exciting world of Lincoln-Douglas debate. Whether your child is brand new to debate or looking to build on their public speaking and critical thinking skills, this camp is the perfect place to start.

Why Choose Our Debate Seminar?

  • Structured, Skill-Building Curriculum
    Our camp offers a clear, step-by-step approach to debate fundamentals—from crafting strong arguments to confident public speaking—ensuring all students make meaningful progress throughout the week.
  • Led by Nationally-Ranked, Experienced Debaters
    Your child will learn from Ayaan Singh and Michael Meng, rising seniors at Strake Jesuit College Preparatory who have qualified for elite tournaments like the Tournament of Champions (TOC), the National Speech and Debate Association (NSDA) National Tournament, and the Texas Forensic Association (TFA) State tournament. They bring expert knowledge and real competitive experience to every session.
  • Convenient, Engaging Virtual Format
    The camp runs fully online, making it accessible from home and designed to keep students engaged through interactive lessons, short mini debates, and group activities that sharpen critical thinking and communication skills.

Camp Schedule

August 4–8, 2025 | Daily from 10:00 AM – 12:00 PM | Fully Virtual – Join from Anywhere!

  • Day 1: Introduction to Lincoln-Douglas Debate & Speaking Fundamentals
    Activities include icebreakers, speaking drills, and persuasive argument brainstorming.
  • Day 2: Case Construction – Building a Solid Argument
    Collaborative case writing and instructor feedback help students craft strong cases.
  • Day 3: Refutation and Cross-Examination
    Learn how to respond to arguments and ask strategic questions through games and practice.
  • Day 4: Round Strategy & Speaking Drills
    Focus on structuring debate rounds, time management, and effective delivery.
  • Day 5: Review, Mini Debates & Next Steps
    Recap skills, participate in short mini-debates, reflect on learning, and receive certificates and awards.
